1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable? The independent variable is type of word - congruent or incongruent. The dependent variable is participants' time to name colors.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices. Let μ c represent the mean time for participants to name congruent colors and let μ i represent the mean time for participants to name incongruent colors. Then,

H o : μ i - μ c = 0

H A : μ i - μ c > 0

The null hypothesis says the average time to name incongruent colors minus the average time to name congruent colors is zero. The alternative hypothesis says that the average time to name incongruent colors is longer than the average time to name congruent colors. I would perform t-tests for dependent samples with α = 0.05 and one-tailed critical values:

  • We want to use t-tests because we want to compare the means of samples that are small (counts less than 30), we do not know the population standard deviations, and the distributions are not highly skewed.
  • We want to use dependent rather than independent sample tests because the samples involve the same participants.
  • We want one-tailed because the alternative hypothesis says the average time to name incongruent colors is greater than that for congruent colors.

6. Optional: What do you think is responsible for the effects observed? Can you think of an alternative or similar task that would result in a similar effect? Some research about the problem will be helpful for thinking about these two questions! I think incongruent colors take longer to identify because the participants have to suppress their knowledge of the meaning of the word. If people were asked to identify shapes that enclosed words such as "triangle", "square," and "star", we can expect them to take longer if the shape does not match the word than if the shape and word were congruent.
